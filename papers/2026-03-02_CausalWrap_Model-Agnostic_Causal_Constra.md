## CausalWrap: Model-Agnostic Causal Constraint Wrappers for Tabular Synthetic Data

### Problem

Standard tabular synthetic data generators (SDGs) are typically trained to match observational distributions, which, while yielding high conventional utility (e.g., column correlations, predictive accuracy), often **fail to preserve structural relations critical for causal analysis and out-of-distribution (OOD) reasoning.** When synthetic data is used for downstream causal tasks (e.g., estimating treatment effects, evaluating policies, testing mediation), merely matching the observational distribution is insufficient; **structural fidelity and treatment-mechanism preservation are essential.**

Existing causal-aware SDG approaches have limitations: they often require a **fully specified causal graph** or address only **specific types of constraints**. In practice, domain experts usually possess **partial causal knowledge (PCK)**—trusted edges, forbidden edges, temporal orderings, or qualitative/monotonic constraints—and practitioners often need to improve the causal properties of **existing, off-the-shelf base generators post-hoc, without access to their internal architecture or training process.**

### Method

CausalWrap (CW) is a **model-agnostic, post-hoc correction wrapper** designed to inject partial causal knowledge (PCK) into samples generated by any pre-trained base tabular generator (e.g., GANs, VAEs, diffusion models) without requiring access to its internals.

1.  **Partial Causal Knowledge (PCK) Framework:** CW formalizes PCK as:
    *   **Trusted Directed Edges (E+):** `Xi` directly causes `Xj`.
    *   **Forbidden Edges (E0):** `Xi` does not directly cause `Xj`.
    *   **Qualitative/Monotonic Constraints (M):** `Xj` is `σ`-monotone in `Xi` given a conditioning set `XS` (`σ` being `+` or `-`).

2.  **Lightweight Correction Map:** CW learns a **differentiable post-hoc correction map** (`fϕ`) that is applied to samples from the frozen base generator. This map transforms raw synthetic samples to enforce causal constraints.

3.  **Augmented Lagrangian Optimization:** The correction map `fϕ` is optimized using an **augmented-Lagrangian schedule**, which combines conventional utility loss with specific causal penalty terms. This adaptive optimization avoids manual tuning of penalty weights and improves convergence.

4.  **Causal Penalty Terms:**
    *   **Residualized Independence Constraints (for E0):** For each forbidden pair `(a, b) ∈ E0`, CW penalizes the dependence between the *residuals* of `Xa` and `Xb` (after regressing out their trusted parents) using the **Hilbert-Schmidt Independence Criterion (HSIC)**. This acts as a differentiable proxy for conditional independence.
    *   **Monotonic/Sign Constraints (for M):** For each monotonic constraint, a **hinge loss** is applied. It evaluates the conditional effect sign by perturbing `Xi` in paired synthetic samples and penalizing sign violations in `Xj`.

CW provides theoretical results connecting penalty-based optimization to constraint satisfaction and relating approximate factorization to joint distributional control.

### Impact

CausalWrap significantly **improves the causal fidelity of synthetic data generated by diverse base models** while largely retaining conventional utility.

*   **Improved Causal Metrics:**
    *   **Reduced Average Treatment Effect (ATE) Error:** Achieved up to **63% reduction** on the ACIC-style semi-synthetic benchmark and improved ATE agreement.
    *   **Increased ATE Agreement:** Lifted ATE agreement from 0.00 to **0.38** on a real-world ICU (MIMIC-IV) cohort where ground truth is unavailable, comparing estimates from synthetic vs. real data across an estimator ensemble.
    *   Improved Conditional ATE (CATE) precision (PEHE) and structural conditional independence pass rate.

*   **Versatility and Applicability:**
    *   Demonstrated effectiveness across three base generator families: **CTGAN (GAN), TabDDPM (Diffusion), and TVAE (VAE)**.
    *   Validated on a range of benchmarks: **simulated Structural Causal Models (SCMs), semi-synthetic causal benchmarks (IHDP, ACIC-style suite), and a real-world ICU cohort (MIMIC-IV)** with expert-elicited partial causal graphs.

*   **Practical Utility:** CausalWrap's model-agnostic, post-hoc nature makes it a highly practical tool for practitioners who want to enhance the causal properties of synthetic data from existing generators without needing to re-architect or retrain them from scratch.
