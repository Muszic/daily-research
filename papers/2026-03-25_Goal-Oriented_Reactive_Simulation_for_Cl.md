# Goal-Oriented Reactive Simulation for Closed-Loop Trajectory Prediction

## Problem

Current autonomous driving trajectory prediction models suffer from significant limitations due to their open-loop training paradigm and reliance on static or non-reactive datasets:

1.  **Covariate Shift and Compounding Errors:** Models trained open-loop struggle in real-world closed-loop deployments. They encounter self-induced states outside their training distribution, leading to compounding prediction errors and reduced safety.
2.  **Lack of Reactive Interaction:** Traditional log-replay or rule-based simulators sever the causal link between the ego agent's actions and the environment's response. This prevents the ego from learning to actively negotiate traffic, leading to passive or exploitative behaviors rather than robust, interactive ones.
3.  **Stability for High-Frequency Replanning:** Rapid, receding horizon replanning, crucial for real-time control, imposes strict stability requirements. Open-loop models are not designed for this, and naive closed-loop adaptation risks collapsing multimodal predictions or introducing severe off-policy bias.

## Method

The paper proposes an on-policy closed-loop training paradigm combined with a novel reactive simulation to address these issues, optimized for high-frequency, receding horizon ego prediction:

1.  **Decoupled Network Architecture:**
    *   **Ego Decoder:** A multimodal transformer-based decoder (using QCNet's query-centric attention and iterative refinement) specifically designed to generate diverse ego trajectory forecasts, enabling flexible downstream planning. It also incorporates a novel full-covariance coordinate transformation for prediction uncertainty.
    *   **Scene Decoder:** A goal-oriented, transformer-based decoder that generates *unimodal, reactive* predictions for surrounding agents. It explicitly conditions surrounding agent behaviors on the ego's state and a randomly sampled future goal token (from ground truth), ensuring plausible and interactive responses.
2.  **On-Policy Closed-Loop Training Strategy:**
    *   The ego agent is trained on a mixture of open-loop data and *self-induced drifted states* generated through on-policy rollouts in a custom PyTorch-native, GPU-accelerated simulator (using Waymax's delta-action dynamics).
    *   Crucially, each *individual ego prediction mode* is optimized solely based on the closed-loop samples generated by its *own* execution, preventing off-policy bias and enabling the model to learn explicit recovery behaviors from its errors.
    *   The simulation employs a `scene simulation mask` to create a hybrid environment, exposing the ego to both reactive (Scene Decoder) and non-reactive (log-replay) surrounding agents, fostering robustness to diverse levels of interactivity.
3.  **Full-Covariance Negative Log Likelihood (NLL) Loss:** A new NLL loss formulation is introduced that explicitly models correlated spatial uncertainty using the Mahalanobis distance and a covariance regularization penalty, improving the robustness and interpretability of multimodal predictions.

## Impact

The proposed closed-loop training paradigm and reactive simulation significantly enhance trajectory prediction capabilities:

1.  **Enhanced Collision Avoidance:** Closed-loop training dramatically reduces collision rates at high replanning frequencies, achieving relative reductions of up to 27.0% on nuScenes and 79.5% in dense DeepScenario intersections compared to open-loop baselines.
2.  **Learned Recovery Behaviors:** The ego model learns to actively correct its own execution errors and stabilize its trajectory from self-induced drifted states, leading to improved safety and robustness under distribution shifts without compromising route adherence.
3.  **Optimal Interactivity-Stability Balance:** The hybrid simulation strategy, combining reactive and non-reactive surrounding agents, successfully optimizes the balance between immediate interactive realism and long-term behavioral stability of the ego agent.
