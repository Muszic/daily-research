### BEEVE: Unsupervised Discovery of Non-Semantic Acoustic States

**Problem:**
Existing bioacoustic methods for discovering structure in biological signals primarily assume vocal production models or predefined semantic units, making them ill-suited for non-vocal species. Honey bee collective buzzing, generated by mechanical muscle vibrations rather than a communicative vocal apparatus, is a prime example of this gap. While evidence suggests these vibrations reflect colony physiological states (e.g., queen status), no current vocal framework applies, hindering the development of truly non-invasive, annotation-free acoustic monitoring for crucial colony health management. The challenge is to discover meaningful, structured acoustic patterns in this non-vocal signal *without any labels or prior semantic assumptions*.

**Method:**
The authors introduce BeeVe, an unsupervised framework for acoustic state discovery in collective honey bee buzzing, applied to approximately five hours of unlabelled hive audio from the UrBAN dataset (annotations used only for data diversity, not training). The method involves two main stages:

1.  **Feature Extraction:** A pre-trained, *frozen* Patchout Spectrogram Transformer (PaSST) (specifically `passt_s_swa_p16_128_ap476` pre-trained on AudioSet) is used as a general acoustic feature extractor. It produces 1295-dimensional embeddings from raw audio at approximately 23ms intervals.
2.  **Acoustic State Discovery:** A Vector-Quantized Variational Autoencoder (VQ-VAE) is trained *entirely without labels* on these PaSST embeddings.
    *   **Encoder:** Compresses the PaSST embeddings into a 128-dimensional continuous latent representation.
    *   **Vector Quantizer:** Maps this continuous representation to the nearest entry in a learned finite discrete codebook of acoustic tokens (with sizes K=32 or K=64). This step effectively discretizes the continuous acoustic space into a vocabulary of recurring patterns. Gradients bypass this non-differentiable step via straight-through estimation, and the codebook is updated using Exponential Moving Average (EMA).
    *   **Decoder:** Reconstructs the original 1295-dimensional PaSST embedding from the chosen discrete codebook entry.
    *   **Training Objective:** The VQ-VAE is optimized using a composite loss function comprising a reconstruction term (MSE) and a quantization term (combining codebook, commitment, and diversity losses), ensuring stable and meaningful discrete representations are learned without any explicit supervision or semantic assumptions.

**Impact:**
BeeVe successfully demonstrates the recovery of repeatable acoustic structure from a non-vocal biological signal without any annotation, opening a novel path for non-invasive hive health monitoring:

*   **Unsupervised State Separation:** Post-hoc evaluation revealed that the learned discrete acoustic tokens effectively separate queenright and queenless colony conditions with high Jensen-Shannon Divergence values between 0.609 and 0.688, despite training entirely without labels.
*   **Granular Sub-state Discovery:** The framework further identified three internally coherent sub-states within the queenless condition, which remained stable across experiments with different codebook sizes and random seeds, suggesting distinct behavioral modes.
*   **Temporal Structure Validation:** Token transition analysis provided strong statistical evidence (p≪0.001) of non-random sequential structure in the learned vocabulary, indicating inherent dynamics in bee buzzing.
*   **Generalizability:** The learned representations and token assignments generalized well to unseen recordings, preserving both token overlap (Jaccard index = 0.947) and global manifold topology.
*   **Novel Framework:** Proposes and experimentally validates a pioneering framework for unsupervised discrete acoustic pattern discovery in a non-vocal species, making no semantic assumptions about the signal, thereby advancing computational intelligence in bioacoustics.
